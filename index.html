Light, Time, and Reality

What is Light?

Light is energy, yet it behaves like both a wave and a particle. It moves at the fastest speed possible in our universe, but it does not experience time.

Timelessness of Light

From a photon's perspective, it is emitted and absorbed instantly, no matter the distance. This means that for light, there is no past, present, or future—only an eternal now.

Spacetime Bends for Light

Einstein’s theory of relativity shows that space and time adjust to keep the speed of light constant. Time slows and space contracts, ensuring that no observer ever sees light moving at anything other than its fixed speed.

Is Light the Fabric of Reality?

All energy and matter originate from light. Some theories suggest that reality itself is structured around photons, making light the most fundamental entity in the universe.

The Simulation Hypothesis

Does light behave like the code of a cosmic simulation? If photons are timeless and omnipresent, could they be the fundamental "pixels" of our reality?
